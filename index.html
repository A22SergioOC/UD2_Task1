The turtles

Turtles are an order of reptiles called Testudines, characterized by a shell developed mainly from their ribs. Turtles have some interesting features, for example:

  • Their shell are made of keratin, a type of protein.
  • The largest living species of turtle is the leatherback turtle, which can reach over 2.7 m in length and weigh over 500 kg.
  • Turtles, like other reptiles, have a limited ability to regulate their body temperature.

Turtles are distributed across the world. Nevertheless, is more frecuently see turtles in different places acording to their type:

  1. Non-marine turtles often live in regions of South Asia, Southeast Asia and the Gulf of Mexico.
  2. Leatherback turtles can be found in colder areas of the Atlantic and Pacific.
  3. Some turtles, for example, the Terrapene ornata are found at high altitudes in New Mexico.
